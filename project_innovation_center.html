Projects Launched from the Innovation Center

Innovation Center Marketing Plan

Problem: An innovation tech noticed that the Innovation U website and Facebook page was underdeveloped and unmarketed, and dedicated time to establish a marketing plan to fix it.

Innovation Center Marketing Graphics

Problem: Nearing the launch of the Innovation Center, the center realized that there was no logo or design motif established for advertising, and so developed an overall graphical design to follow.

Innovation U Student Portraits

Problem: The Innovation Center wanted prospective students and community partners to know about the significance of Innovation Education for students.

MJC and LAO Youth Empowerment Project

Problem: The LAO Youth Empowerment Project wanted to establish a platform to address the school to prison pipeline in West Modesto.

Letters Home Photography Project

Problem: A number of different immigrant and refugee groups have made new homes in the Central Valley, and wanted to establish a cultural connection and understanding with already established residents.

Sewing Circle

Problem: Since the US withdrawal from Afghanistan, Stanislause County has become home to an up-and-coming community of Afghan women - many of whom love to sew but have no machines.

LAO Programming Group

Problem: The "college fear factor" and general unfamiliarity with higher education are very real challenges that prevent many community youths from envisioning themselves as future college students.

Nursing Organization Project

Problem: The nursing students in learning in Glacier Hall of MJC have to make due with worn down and old class materials due to the high cost of medical equipment. They requested that Innovation Center technicians design and create copies of those supplies that would save hundreds to thousands of dollars.

The Biomass Research Workshop and Fellowship

Problem: How can MJC prepare students to be involved in the burgeoning biomass industry in the Northern San Joaquin?

MJC Biofuels

How can Modesto Junior College students reduce the environmental footprint of their campus and strengthen the sustainable impact of MJC for our community?
