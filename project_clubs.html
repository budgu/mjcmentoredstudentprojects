Projects Launched from Clubs

Hambastagi Zanan

Problem: With over 250 Afghan women refugee students at MJC, many of whom are new arrivals, it is important for students to be able to access resources and linguistically and culturally-relevant support to address pressing barriers to education and their own engagement in the community.

MJC Music Ambassadors and Mentorship

Problem: A lack of access to music instruction, instruments, and private instruction in areas of music that are of most interest to the youth of the community combined with a lack of opportunities to explore and become knowledgeable about possible educational & career paths in music.

Every Campus A Refugee Program

Problem: Resettlement and integration of newcomers into the local community needs a lot of support. Refugee resettlement agencies, such as World Relief, are given only ninety days to support new refugees with integration. The needs of newcomers are significant and challenging, and often overwhelm resettlement agency staff.
