Hambastagi Zanan

Students: Shabnam Mujaddidi, Shamim Mujaddidi, Laila Noorani, Ghazal Noorani, Farishta Shams

Mentors: Ruth Luman

Problem: With over 250 Afghan women refugee students at MJC, many of whom are new arrivals, it is important for students to be able to access resources and linguistically and culturally-relevant support to address pressing barriers to education and their own engagement in the community.

Solution: To this end, the MJC hambastagi zanan (meaning 'women united' in Dari) was begun by recently-arrived Afghan women students. Drawing on the cultural institution of women's associations to encourage women to gather, share resources, build community, and find solutions to collective struggles, the hambastagi zanan met regularly over fall and spring semesters. During these gatherings, women hambastagi leaders brought in community and college guests to share financial, legal, entrepreneurial, and academic resources. Identifying the need to network, the women have worked with the founders of a community networking app, Abule, to make it more linguistically relevant to Afghan women. The women also identified the lack of access to driving education as a major barrier to engaging fully in school and the community. To that end, the women organized a series of English for Driving workshops with the possibility of receiving free behind-the-wheel driving lessons from local driving schools.
