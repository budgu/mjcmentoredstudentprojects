Letters Home

Student:Thelma Saldivar

Mentors:Ruth Luman (English Language, MJC) and Noah Wilson (Photography, MJC)

Problem:A number of different immigrant and refugee newcomer groups have made their home in the Central Valley. Many long-time residents witness this resettlement but do not always connect or empathize with the personal experiences of these newcomers and see the cultural wealth they bring.

Solution:Thelma Saldivar, an MJC photography major, wanted to expand her experiences in photography and find a way to tell the story of newcomers to the community through art. The Letters Home Photography project is the visual documentation of these resettlement stories of immigrant and refugee college students. The photographs represent their letters to students' families in their native countries about their new life and the longings for and ties to a cultural heritage they wish to maintain. The project had its inaugural debut during ModShop with over 500 visitors and subsequent exhibitions at The Mistlin Gallery, the IllList at the State Theater, The Swimmers film screening at The State, and most recently during MJC's Innovation Showcase. The exhibition project launched not only the story of resettlement into wider community consciousness, but also the confidence and career of a promising photography student.
