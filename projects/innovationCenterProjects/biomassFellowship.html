The Biomass Research Workshop and Fellowship

Students: Daniel Castillo, Gustavo Demoner, Finn Ceja, Jamie Jiminez, Helen Nguyen, Cody Dowdell, Michael Dark, Laurence Tabangcura, Immanuel Pogutter, Stephanie Barajas, Alfredo Ojeda, Gabi Pate

Mentors: Professor Deborah Gilbert

Problem: How can MJC prepare students to be involved in the burgeoning biomass industry in the Northern San Joaquin?

Solution: The more we, MJC students and faculty, learn about biomass in the region and the challenges around converting it, the better equipped we are to get involved. In August 2023, twelve community college students worked like graduate students for three intensive weeks and created a Biomass Challenge Bank with the purpose of establishing a starting point for future mentored student projects focused on biomass conversion. This work aligns with a regional initiative, Stan2030, and specifically BEAM Circular's well-resourced efforts to establishing a new economic hub in the Northern San Joaquin Valley. Students practiced elements of a human centered design training, heard from eight guest speakers, interviewed 27 stakeholders, took two field trips (to Aemetis methane conversion plant and the Almond Board's Biomass committee meeting). Eight of the ten continued work on mentored and paid projects that grew from the challenge bank. We started a biocircular initiative rolling on our campus.
