Problem:Local history in our community is very limited. We have a few places where parts of our story is told, but it isnâ€™t very comprehensive. Our project is the beginning of a long-term term effort to fill in the gaps in our knowledge of our collective past.

Solution: This is a public history project focusing on the local history of Stanislaus County in the 1920s. We recruited nine MJC students to learn how to do local history research, then they explored primary source documents and created a museum exhibit. We decided on this project as a first start to exhuming local history that is not easily found in our local museums or public spaces. The exhibit now resides in Founders Hall on MJC's East Campus, where students, faculty and staff can learn new stories about our collective past. We are currently working with a new group of students on another aspect of our local history: redlining and housing segregation in the 1930s and 1940s. We are collaborating with community groups, MJC faculty and the MJC Democracy in the Public Space lecture series to present our findings and offer solutions this fall.
