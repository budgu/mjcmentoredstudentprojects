Shuttle Soap Dispensers

Students: Students in Engineering 100 Class (Intro to Engineering)

Mentors: Jeremy Wilson (Engineering, MJC)

Problem:The Shower Shuttle, the mobile shower service that brings hot showers to the regionâ€™s homeless population had an issue with their shampoo dispensers. The lids were being bumped off during use and cracking after hitting the floor of the shower. The lids are not available individually, so it was necessary to purchase new dispensers each time a lid cracked. Dean Dodd, founder of the Shower Shuttle, approached Professor Deborah Gilbert about the problem who mentioned it to Jeremy Wilson, professor of Engineering.

Solution: Professor Wilson brought the problem to the students of his Intro to Engineering course. Each of the students designed a replacement lid in Fusion 360, a 3D design program for mechanical parts. In early 2023, the first prototype was 3D printed and delivered for trial use. The MJC Innovation Center will continue to provide 3D printed lids as needed for the Shower Shuttle.
