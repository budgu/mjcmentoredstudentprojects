Problem: Most newcomer Afghan women have never had the experience of sitting in the front seat of a car much less practice driving on a freeway. Given that driving is a necessity in the Central Valley and behind-the-wheel practice and instruction is often expensive with a commercial driving school, many women have found it difficult to get the practice they need, especially as community volunteers are reluctant to teach women who do not know English.

Solution: Students and faculty piloted workshops around English for driving to help the women develop enough basic English language skills to get them a literal driverâ€™s seat with a community volunteer instructor. The pilot then turned into an official course offered through the college and was recently mentioned in a front-page New York Times article.
